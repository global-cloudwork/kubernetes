1. Intentionality
The ability to form intentions and goals.
This means deciding what you want to do before acting.

2. Motivation
The internal drive or reasons behind actions.
Without motivation, intentions rarely become actions.

3. Autonomy
The freedom to make choices without excessive external constraint.
This includes independence, self-direction, and control over one’s actions.

4. Capability / Competence
The skills, knowledge, and resources needed to act effectively.
Agency requires the capacity to carry out decisions, not just the desire.

5. Self-efficacy
The belief in one’s ability to achieve desired outcomes.
People with higher self-efficacy are more likely to act and persist.

6. Action
Concrete steps taken toward a goal.
Agency is ultimately expressed through behavior, not just intention.

7. Reflexivity / Self-awareness
The ability to reflect on one’s choices, circumstances, and identity.
This includes understanding how external forces shape one’s decisions.

8. Influence
The ability for actions to affect one’s environment or outcomes.
Agency includes having impact, not simply trying.
